The cats

11.7" x 16.5"
Oil pastel on paper
March 2009

This is the piece that I sent to an art competition for kids in Ho Chi Minh City when I was in third grade. I drew it totally under my art teacher's instructions, so a lot of ideaes and details here did not come from the nine-year-old me. At that time, I thought I would win a high prize with this piece. Thankfully, the judge recognized this, and awarded me only the a small prize. I was disappointed at first, but later, this experience gave me a priceless lesson: In art, just be ourselves!

The hawker's
New Year's Eve

16" x 24"
Water color on fabric canvas
January 2014

"New Year is coming! Come, buy your fruits! Come, buy your flowers!"
In my country, everytime around Lunar New Year, all the hawkers went down the streets. Their rhythmic cries echoed everywhere, from alleys to avenues, making the whole city full of the joys of spring. Never did they stop their walks. Never did they lose their smiles. What they brought me was not only goods, but also their sense of optimism in the midst of hardship. All these feelings and images have unconsciously became a part of Tet that I would never forget.

My grandma and I

16" x 24"
Water color on fabric canvas
May 2013

My grandma has always been the closest to me. Kindergarten, she walked with me. Elementary, she played with me. Junior high schohol, she learned with me. Senior high school, she dreamed with me. Everyday, she would wake me up, walk me from home to school, and from school back to home. Everyday, I would eate the rice she cooks, wear the clothes she irons, and hear the songs she sings. I do not want to know how much time is left, but I pray to God everyday that she can live with me until the end of this world.
